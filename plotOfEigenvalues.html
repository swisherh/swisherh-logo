The L-function associated to a Maass form on \(SL(3,Z)\) satisfies a functional equation of the form

\begin{align} \Lambda(s) =& \pi^{-3s/2} \Gamma\left(\frac{s}{2} + \mu_1 i\right) \Gamma\left(\frac{s}{2} + \mu_2 i\right) \Gamma\left(\frac{s}{2} - (\mu_1 +\mu_2) i\right) L(s)\cr =& \overline{\Lambda(1 - \bar{s})}, \end{align}

where the \(\mu_j\) are real. By relabeling, rearranging, and conjugating, we may assume \(0\le \mu_2 \le \mu_1\).

Note: a better normalization replaces our \(\mu_j\) by \(2\mu_j\). We are in the process of converting our data.
